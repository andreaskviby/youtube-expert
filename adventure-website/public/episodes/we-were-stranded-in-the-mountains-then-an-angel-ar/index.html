We Were Stranded in the Mountains... Then an Angel Arrived 🇮🇹❤️(#53)

Published: April 16, 2026

🇮🇹 What started as a wonderful day exploring Sicily quickly turned into one of the most frightening experiences we've had since moving here.
After visiting Corleone and enjoying the breathtaking landscapes of inland Sicily, everything suddenly changed.
Far from towns, far from help, and surrounded by mountains, we found ourselves stranded in a situation that became more serious with every passing minute.
For most people, it would have been stressful.
For Andreas, who has Type 1 diabetes, it became something much more alarming.
When you're dependent on insulin and careful planning every day, unexpected situations can quickly become dangerous. As the hours passed and options seemed limited, we realized just how vulnerable we really were.
This could have ended very differently.
But sometimes, when things look darkest, someone appears who restores your faith in humanity.
His name was Antonio.
A complete stranger who stepped in when we needed help the most.
An angel in Sicily.
❤️
His kindness, generosity, and willingness to help reminded us why we fell in love with this island in the first place. Sicily is not just about beautiful landscapes, historic towns, and incredible food.
It's about the people.
The moments of unexpected kindness.
The human connections that stay with you long after the day is over.
And after a day like this, there was only one way to celebrate.
Pizza.
🍕✨
Back home in Termini Imerese, we visited one of our favorite places: Tocca a Tia.
More than just a restaurant or pizzeria, Tocca a Tia represents something special. It combines outstanding Sicilian food with values that matter: inclusion, community, local identity, and a genuine passion for bringing people together.
Using quality local ingredients and supporting social engagement within the community, it has become a place that reflects the very best of modern Sicily.
A place where food brings people together.
A place where strangers become friends.
And after the day we'd just experienced, it felt like the perfect ending.
Join us for a journey through the mountains of Sicily, an unexpected crisis, an unforgettable act of kindness, and a reminder that sometimes the most important part of travel isn't the places you visit...
It's the people you meet along the way.
