Beach Guide

Our favorite swimming spots in Sicily, honestly reviewed

We've explored over 1,000 km of Sicilian coastline. Here are the spots we keep coming back to — honestly reviewed by expats who actually swim here year-round.

Mazzaforno Beach, Cefalù

8 / 10

A sheltered little bay just west of Cefalù that's well worth a stop. The water is calm, clear, and warm, which makes it ideal for snorkeling — head for the rocky edges of the bay and you'll find plenty of fish rather than the seaweed in the middle. There's a free public stretch if you just want to throw down a towel, plus a paid section where you can rent sunbeds and umbrellas. A few bars and restaurants right on the sand sort you out for a cold drink, a panino, or a slice of pizza without leaving the beach, and there are lifeguards, showers, and toilets too. Knock off a couple of points for limited space and parking that fills up fast — but arrive early and it's a brilliant spot.

Directions

From Cefalù old town, head west on the SS113 for about 3 km; the bay is signposted near the seafront campsite. If you're on the A20 Palermo–Messina motorway, take the Cefalù exit, follow signs toward the town, then continue west on the SS113. You can park along the road on either side of the beach, but it's tight, so get there early.

Beach Tips for Sicily

  • Go early or late - Beaches are empty before 10am and after 5pm
  • Bring water shoes - Many beaches are rocky, not sandy
  • August is crazy - All of Italy is on holiday, beaches are packed
  • Look for "spiaggia libera" - Free public beaches between the paid lidos
  • Pack a picnic - Some of the best beaches have no facilities
