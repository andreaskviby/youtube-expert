🏺 Syracuse & Southeast

Ancient history meets baroque beauty

Why Syracuse?

Syracuse (Siracusa) was once the most powerful city in the ancient world - larger than Athens. Today, the island of Ortigia is one of Italy's most beautiful historic centers. The southeast is also home to the baroque towns of Noto, Ragusa, and Modica, all UNESCO World Heritage sites.

This region has some of Sicily's best beaches, excellent wine (Nero d'Avola country), and a more relaxed pace than the big cities.

Buying Property in Syracuse

The southeast has become popular with international buyers, especially around Noto and Ragusa. Prices have risen but remain reasonable. Ortigia is more expensive but offers that unique island-within-a-city lifestyle.

  • Ortigia: Premium prices, €100,000+ for apartments
  • Noto area: Growing market, beautiful countryside
  • Ragusa/Modica: Great value, stunning baroque towns
  • Rural southeast: Farmhouses and masserie from €50,000

What to Know

  • Climate: Hot and dry summers, very mild winters
  • Transport: Catania Airport is closest (1 hour), limited trains
  • Character: Relaxed, artistic, food and wine focused
  • Challenges: Car essential in rural areas, summer tourism crowds in Ortigia
