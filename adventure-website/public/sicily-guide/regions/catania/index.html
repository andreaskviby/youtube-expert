🌋 Catania & East Coast

Living in the shadow of Mount Etna

Why Catania?

Catania is Sicily's second-largest city, rebuilt in stunning baroque style after the 1693 earthquake. It sits at the foot of Mount Etna, Europe's most active volcano, giving the region a unique character - black lava stone buildings, incredibly fertile soil, and that dramatic backdrop.

The fish market (La Pescheria) is legendary. The street food is some of Sicily's best. And the airport makes it easy to reach from anywhere in Europe.

Buying Property in Catania

The East Coast offers diverse options - from apartments in Catania's historic center to rural properties on Etna's slopes. Villages like Zafferana Etnea, Linguaglossa, and Randazzo offer mountain living with stunning views.

  • City apartments: From €50,000 for renovation projects
  • Etna villages: Houses from €30,000-€100,000
  • Coastal properties: Higher prices near Taormina

What to Know

  • Climate: Hot summers, mild winters, occasional Etna snowfall at altitude
  • Transport: Catania-Fontanarossa Airport (CTA), good train connections
  • Character: Urban energy, university town, great nightlife
  • Challenges: Traffic in the city, volcanic activity (mostly harmless but dramatic)
